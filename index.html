Week 5

  • Searched for more concepts in aerodynamics, one of the sources used was Wind turbines analysis book, and some examples of the concepts are:
    • The theoretical generated electricity by VAWTs: E = ½ρAV3, where:
      • (p) is the density of the surrounding fluid.
      • (A) is the swept area, which is the area of the circle formed by the turbine blades as they rotate.
      • (V) is the Relative wind velocity to the blades, as it is the real velocity experienced by the blade.
    • The Betz limit, CP, max = 16/27 = 0.5926, which is the maximum theoretically possible rotor power coefficient.
    • Betz limit, and the assumptions it considers, like:
      • Homogenous, incompressible, steady state fluid flow.
      • No frictional drag.
      • An infinite number of blades.
      • Uniform thrust over the disc or rotor area.
      • A non-rotating wake.
      • The static pressure far upstream and far downstream of the rotor is equal to the undisturbed ambient static pressure.
    • In practice, three effects lead to a decrease in the maximum achievable power coefficient, which are:
      • Rotation of the wake behind the rotor.
      • finite number of blades and associated tip losses.
      • non-zero aerodynamic drag.
    • The derivative of Betz limit.
